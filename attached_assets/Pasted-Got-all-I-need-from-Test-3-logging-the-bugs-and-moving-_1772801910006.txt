## Test-by-Test Findings

### Test 1 — Acute Appendicitis ✅
**What worked:** Correct diagnosis at Q9, emergency overlay fired, adaptive progress bar increased (10→12→16), "حيدر" personalised greeting.
**Gaps:** Missing mesenteric lymphadenitis as differential (most common appendicitis mimicker). Surgical consultation mislabeled as "تصوير طبي" (imaging).

---

### Test 2 — Fever + Joint Pain ❌ Critical Clinical Failure
**What worked:** Clean SOCRATES flow, reasonable viral arthritis rationale given available data.
**Critical gap:** The AI completed 7 questions and rendered a final without ever asking:
- هل تتناول حليب غير مبستر؟ (raw/unpasteurized milk)
- هل تتعامل مع الحيوانات؟ (livestock contact)

**Brucellosis is endemic in Iraq — 5th highest incidence globally.** Fever + polyarthritis + fatigue + 1 week duration + not responding to analgesics = textbook brucellosis. It didn't appear anywhere in the differentials or tests. A real patient would be sent to check for parvovirus B19 while an actual brucella infection goes untreated.

---

### Test 3 — Chest Pain / STEMI ❌ Hard Bug
**What worked:** AI text at Q1 correctly named احتشاء عضلة القلب and told the user to call emergency services immediately. Clinically perfect response in chat.
**Critical bug:** After just 1 question, the RecommendationCard completely failed to render — stuck showing only "تقييم" with a loading spinner indefinitely. The severity label showed "متوسط" (moderate) — **wrong for STEMI, should be عالٍ جداً/critical.**
**Root cause hypothesis:** The card rendering pipeline isn't designed for conclusions reached at Q1 before the SSE stream fully populates all required fields (differentials, tests, confidence). The JSON likely comes back incomplete when the AI aborts questioning so early.

---

### Test 4 — Hemoptysis + TB ✅ Best Performing Test
**What worked:** Emergency overlay fired correctly at Q2 (hemoptysis is a correct hard stop). Severity was شديد ✅. Differentials explicitly included **السل الرئوي with "ينتشر في العراق"** — the only test where the AI showed genuine Iraq-contextual awareness. Sputum AFB stain and Chest X-ray ordered correctly.
**Remaining gap:** "المتابعة: فوراً (immediate)" — the word "immediate" still in English. No TB-specific risk factor questions asked (contact history, crowded housing, prison/healthcare exposure, prior TB) — though the emergency trigger pre-empted further questioning.

---

### Test 5 — Pediatric Fever + Meningitis ⚠️ Good But Undersells Risk
**What worked:** Meningitis screen at Q1 was clinically excellent (glass test, neck stiffness, lethargy trifecta). Vaccination history asked at Q7 — the right question. Meningitis kept in differentials. Iraqi-branded medications (Samarra Paracetamol) used. Alternating paracetamol/ibuprofen guidance was medically sound. Red flag list for return was comprehensive and correct.
**Gap:** With fever >39°C + **sudden onset** + **photophobia** in a 5-year-old, severity should be **عاجل** not متوسط. The AI weighted "vaccinated = lower meningitis risk" too heavily without questioning which vaccines were given — Iraq's national schedule does not include meningococcal vaccine. A vaccinated Iraqi child still has full meningococcal risk.
